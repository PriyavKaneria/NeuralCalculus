happiness is incompressible.

if you are happy, someone else has to be sad. They are independent events but total happiness is always constant across the globe or even for a smaller set of people when observed in isolation.

never take happiness for granted.

cumulative happiness always seems to approach 0 which implies you can not be happy all the time in theory. if you are -2 happy for 5 days a +10 might be on the way, and vice versa if a +10 happens first some -5s can be expected.
but it's not that straightforward as the units scale over time. a +10 today might be +4 by tomorrows standards so the scale needs to be applied before applying the cummulative sum to get a weighted cummulative happiness sum.

lots of small failures are ideal.

when failures are inevitable, lots of them are hetter than a few large ones. for success it's a choice whether lots of small one's are good enough or a big one is needed. for most people this choice comes by luck, for some it may be a concious choice
